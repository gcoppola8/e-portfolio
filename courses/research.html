Unit 1

Discussion

ACM highlights a case of abusive workplace environment, in such case Diane is prevented to collaborate fairly to the project as her peers do. Her contributions are not recognized or cited and the behavior of her technical leader is known to be not professional when a mistake is made, this is especially noticeable when addressing female workers and possibly other groups of workers. Diane reported the situation to her manager Jean that minimized the episodes reported and doing so decriminalizing the detrimental behavior and de facto accepting them in the workplace environment. 

 As in (Ebeid et al., 2011) "An organization becomes abusive when it permits or tolerates abusive employee treatment by supervisors or managers" and this is exactly the case when Jean didn't take action. 


Recognize and punish organizations that implement abusive methodologies can be difficult, this because law jurisdiction could be limited or because the abuse can be performed as an apparent innocent jest especially by skilled abusive supervisor. 

 Instead an effective strategy would be to encourage employees to report abusive incidents, implement reviews for supervisors and reward based on these. (Ebeid et al., 2011) Communicate to employees and supervisors the importance of a positive workplace environment (Ebeid et al., 2011) for the organization productivity and financial view point.


References:
Ebeid, Fred & Kaul, Tej & Neumann, Kathleen & Shane, Hugh. (2011). Workplace Abuse: Problems And Consequences. International Business & Economics Research Journal

Unit 5

Inappropriate Use of Surveys

The case of Cambridge-Analytica in 2018 has been the biggest data scandal on social media until today. Specifically, the data of 87 millions of users on Facebook that have been collected for academical research purpose where then used to run political campaign and influence democratic processes as elections in United States. The case generated a series of consequences for Facebook as well. The problem is both ethical and legal, but complex to prevent and manage. Was Facebook guilty? Was Cambridge-Analytica doing something illegal? How can we monitor users data and how they are used once these are shared with third-party?
These are the kind of questions that this and similar accidents arise.
