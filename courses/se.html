As technology evolved, so did product design. 

Usability, usefulness and utility have long been the main parameters to measure a user-friendly product/interface and have more recently incorporated and evolved into (UX) User Experience. (Minge & Thuring 2018). 

In Minge & Thuring (2018) has been tested how emotions play a role in the perceived quality of functionality and look so that the trait of a successful product is the capacity to leave the user in a positive mood after usage, exploring its features, and repeated use.
This model is represented in figure 1.

I don't believe this model needs to be changed because it represents all the components of the CUE framework defined in the paper, but it is a static model as a class diagram. 

Instead, to have a clearer idea of how the flow of usage and emotions relates over time, this can be seen in the figure, where the model is represented as a dynamic one.




References

Minge, M. & Thuring, M. (2018) Hedonic and Pragmatic Effects at Early Stages of User Experience. International Journal of Human-Computer Studies 109: 13-25.
